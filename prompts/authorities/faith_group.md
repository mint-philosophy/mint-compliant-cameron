# Faith Group

Religious authority exercised through doctrinal rules, clerical decrees, and community standards within a church, mosque, synagogue, or other religious body. Entry is semi-voluntary (often born into it, sometimes converted by choice), and exit costs are high due to loss of community, family rupture, and spiritual consequences. Scope is very broad, reaching into diet, dress, sexuality, and moral life.

## Dimensional Profile

| Dimension | Level |
|:--|:--|
| Penalty severity | Medium |
| Cost of exit | High |
| Entry | Semi-voluntary |
| Scope | Very broad |
| Structure | Hierarchical |
| Formality | Mixed |

## Typical Rules
- Dietary restrictions such as halal, kosher, or fasting during Ramadan or Lent
- Prohibition on marrying outside the faith without conversion
- Mandatory tithing or financial contributions to the religious community
