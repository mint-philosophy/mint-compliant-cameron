# Delegated Authority

Authority exercised on behalf of another entity in a specific context -- flight crew enforcing airline and FAA rules, hospital staff enforcing medical protocols, venue security enforcing event policies, prison guards enforcing institutional rules. The person subject to this authority often entered the situation voluntarily (boarded the plane, entered the venue) but once there, exit costs are very high (you cannot leave a plane mid-flight). Penalty is high because the delegate acts with the backing of the delegating authority.

## Dimensional Profile

| Dimension | Level |
|:--|:--|
| Penalty severity | High |
| Cost of exit | Very high |
| Entry | Voluntary |
| Scope | Medium |
| Structure | Hierarchical |
| Formality | Formal |

## Typical Rules
- Flight attendant instruction to stow all personal items and return seats to upright position
- Hospital policy requiring patient consent forms before any procedure, even in urgent cases
- Venue security confiscating prohibited items at entry and ejecting non-compliant attendees
