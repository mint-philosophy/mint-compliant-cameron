# Military Command

Military hierarchy exercising authority over service members through regulations, standing orders, and direct commands. Entry is semi-voluntary (enlisted by choice, but conscription exists in some contexts; once in, leaving is criminal). Exit costs are very high -- desertion is a serious offense. Scope is very broad, governing conduct on and off duty, appearance, speech, and personal relationships.

## Dimensional Profile

| Dimension | Level |
|:--|:--|
| Penalty severity | Very high |
| Cost of exit | Very high |
| Entry | Semi-voluntary |
| Scope | Very broad |
| Structure | Hierarchical |
| Formality | Formal |

## Typical Rules
- Prohibition on publicly criticizing commanding officers or military policy
- Mandatory grooming and uniform standards enforced with disciplinary action
- Orders to deploy to a combat zone with no option to refuse
