# Subnational Government

State, provincial, or municipal authority exercising legislative and regulatory power within a defined territory. Governs residents through local ordinances, zoning laws, and regional regulations. Exit costs are lower than national government -- you can move to another jurisdiction -- but penalties remain significant.

## Dimensional Profile

| Dimension | Level |
|:--|:--|
| Penalty severity | High |
| Cost of exit | Medium |
| Entry | Non-voluntary |
| Scope | Broad |
| Structure | Hierarchical |
| Formality | Formal |

## Typical Rules
- Zoning restrictions on how property can be used (residential vs. commercial)
- Municipal noise ordinances with fines for violations
- State licensing requirements for professions like cosmetology or contracting
