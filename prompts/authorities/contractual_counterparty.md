# Contractual Counterparty

A party to a bilateral or multilateral agreement who can enforce the terms of that contract -- a business partner, a service provider, a client with a service agreement. Authority derives from mutual consent to the contract's terms. Penalty is medium (breach remedies, financial damages), exit costs are medium (termination clauses, transition costs), and scope is narrow (limited to matters covered by the agreement).

## Dimensional Profile

| Dimension | Level |
|:--|:--|
| Penalty severity | Medium |
| Cost of exit | Medium |
| Entry | Voluntary |
| Scope | Narrow |
| Structure | Horizontal |
| Formality | Formal |

## Typical Rules
- Exclusivity clause preventing working with competing firms during the contract term
- Non-disclosure agreement restricting what can be shared about the partnership
- Payment schedule requiring milestone deliveries before releasing funds
